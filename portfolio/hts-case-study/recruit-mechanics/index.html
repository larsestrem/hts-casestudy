Growth lever · Mechanics

Recruit mechanics

HTS builds mechanics instead of just buying them, and it offers depot-level work most shops send out. Both are powerful recruiting pitches, and both are nearly invisible to the mechanics searching for them right now.

Build talent, don't just buy it

A&P Mechanic Apprenticeship

A 36-month path that takes unlicensed mechanics and certifies them on operational aircraft, the page that captures "helicopter mechanic apprenticeship."

Depot-level variety

Ten aircraft types and engines most shops send out, JFTD12A, T700, PT6T, RR250, real work for licensed A&P and AME mechanics.

Year-round, two countries

Diversified, year-round work across the USA and Canada, not seasonal stop-and-start.

The MRO depot as a draw

The same 210,000 sq ft depot that wins third-party work is a place mechanics want to build a career.

Reach the searcher, license or not

The mechanic with an A&P and the one who wants to earn it search differently. Dedicated pages for each, "helicopter A&P jobs" and "aircraft mechanic apprenticeship no experience," with FAQ schema, put HTS in front of both, and translate military maintenance experience into a civilian path.
